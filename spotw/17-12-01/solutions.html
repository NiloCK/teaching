The idea here is to identify some feature(s) of the folded-up house that can be tested against the available answers. Since there are only a few feaures to the house, we can concentrate on them:

The door is on a wall with a triangular top. This can eliminate house 5.

Turning your head (or the piece of paper) upside-down and imagining the walls folding together, we see that the black dot is to the left of the door when we are looking at the door.

Houses 2 and 3 can be eliminated because the dot isn't where it should be relative to the door. House 4 can be eliminated because the door isn't where it must be relative to the dot.

This leaves house 1.

Using place-value knowledge, first attemps to find the closest future year with this property fail: 2018 and 2019 will have sums that are too large, because the 7 has been increased while the other digits stayed the same.

Next, incrementing the tens place by one (2027) we have a sum of 11. To balance out this change, we remove one from the ones place, giving $2026$.

3.

Finding the 'lone' digit directly is more difficult than first learning about the repeated digit. Eg, if we guess that the lone digit might be $5$, then we're left with the $4$ repeated digits totaling $43 - 5 = 38$, which is difficult to parse because $4$ doesn't divide $38$ evenly.

Instead, trying to guess the repeated digit will give more information. Maybe it's $5$ - then the sum of these repeated digits is $20$, which means the last digit must be ... $23$.

Since $23$ is not a digit, we can tell that the repeated digits must account for more of the sum. Adjusting this guess upward, we find that the repeated digit can only be $9$, and the lone digit is $43 - 4*9 = 43 - 36 = 7$.

  • Substituting an alternate question / taking an alternate route
  • Trial, Error, and Adjustment
