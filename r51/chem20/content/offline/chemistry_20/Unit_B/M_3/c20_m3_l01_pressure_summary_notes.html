Module 3—Behaviour of Gases

Pressure—Summary Notes
  • Pressure is the force exerted over the area of a surface.

  • Atmospheric pressure is the pressure exerted by the air. At sea level this is 101.325 kPa, 1 atmosphere (atm), 760 mm Hg, 760 torr.

  • kPa is read as kilopascals, the unit of pressure in SI.

  • STP, standard temperature and pressure, is a description of a set of conditions for a gas: 101.325 kPa and 0°C.

  • SATP, standard ambient temperature and pressure, is another set of conditions: 100 kPa and 25°C.
