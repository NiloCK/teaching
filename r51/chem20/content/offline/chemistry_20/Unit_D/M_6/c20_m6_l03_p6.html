Module 6—Stoichiometry

Lesson Summary

 

 In this lesson you explored the following essential question:

  • How is the stoichiometric method applied to reactions that involve gases?

You learned to apply your knowledge of stoichiometry to predict the quantities of gaseous substances involved in chemical reactions. You also completed a laboratory experiment where hydrogen gas was produced in a chemical reaction.  You then saw examples of how knowledge of the stoichiometry of gases is important to the operation of many technologies, including combustion processes and hydrogen-oxygen fuel cells.

 

In the next lesson you will look at the stoichiometry of reactions that involve solutions.
