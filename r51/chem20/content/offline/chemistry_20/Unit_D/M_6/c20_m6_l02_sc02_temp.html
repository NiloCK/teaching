 Lab: Decomposition of Malachite
Predicted mass of copper(II) oxide that will remain when 2.00 g of malachite undergoes decomposition when heated.

mmalachite  = 2.000 g

Mmalachite = 221.13 g/mol

nCuO = ?

MCuO = 79.55 g/mol

nmalachite = 2.000 g/221.13 g/mol = 0.009 044 453 5 molmalachite

nCuO = 0.009 044 453 5 molmalachite x 2CuO/1malachite = 0.018 088 907 molCuO

mCuO = 0.018 088 907 molCuO x 79.55 g/mol = 1.438 972 55 g = 1.439 g

 
a. mass of an empty Erlenmeyer flask (g) 91.78
b. mass of the Erlenmeyer flask with malachite (g) 93.78
Subtract (a) from (b) to obtain the mass of malachite (g). 2.00
c. mass of Erlenmeyer flask with CuO (g) 93.22
Subtract (a) from (c) to obtain the mass of CuO produced (g). 93.22 - 91.78 = 1.44
